Visualizations — firm-side lag profiles

β coefficients (with 95% CI bands) from the anchor SSIV reduced-form spec, plotted against the FX-shifter lag (years 0–10). Six firm-side outcome families across the two NEC datasets: annual flow (NEC panel) and 2018 stock (NEC cross-section). Each figure contains an embedded methodological footnote.

The plots below show how firm-side outcomes respond to the lagged FX-shifter × baseline-migrant-intensity interaction. The flow plots come from the NEC firm panel (muni × founding-year cells, 2001–2018). The stock plots come from the 2018 NEC cross-section, with three overlays where applicable: (a) the full 2018 stock, (b) firms founded 2001–2018 surviving to 2018, and (c) firms founded 2011–2018 — to separate the cohort effect from incumbent-clearing. All specs use the anchor controls (year × mig_int_z, year × fx_z, Block A, district FE; SE clustered at muni), sample restricted to munis with ≥ 25 baseline migrants in 2001.

A. Firm flow (NEC panel, annual entry)

1. Annual firm entry by industry — lag profile

log(1 + new firms) in muni m founded in year t, for 7 ISIC industries plus the aggregate. Hospitality & food shows the deepest negative response (lag 2–5); transport, trade-retail, and construction follow milder negative patterns; agriculture and finance/prof/RE drift positive at mid-lags.

Source: output/tab/robustness_nec.csv (NEC panel) · SVG

2. Annual firm entry by size — lag profile

Same flow spec, decomposed by 2018-observed firm size (1 / 2–9 / 10–50 / 51+ workers) plus aggregate. The negative aggregate effect comes mainly from suppressed 2–9 worker firm formation; solo (1-worker) entry is roughly flat; 10–50 worker entry is too rare for clean identification.

Source: output/tab/robustness_nec.csv (NEC panel) · SVG
B. Firm stock (NEC 2018 cross-section) — composition

3. Industry composition in 2018 — lag profile, by cohort

Muni-level share of firms in 8 grouped industries. Three datasets overlaid: full 2018 stock; post-2001 cohort; post-2011 cohort. Hospitality share consistently negative (≈ −1.0 pp); agriculture share consistently positive (≈ +0.5 pp). Composition effects are immediate, not cumulative.

Source: output/tab/robustness_nec.csv + robustness_nec_cohort.csv · SVG

4. Size composition in 2018 — lag profile, by cohort

Muni-level share of 2018 firms in each size bucket. Headline shift: +1.5 pp positive on 1-worker firms balanced by ≈ −1.5 pp negative on 2–9 workers — shocks reallocate the firm distribution toward solo proprietors. Cohort restriction lifts the 51+ worker share slightly but the effect remains tiny (≤ +0.1 pp).

Source: output/tab/robustness_nec.csv + robustness_nec_cohort.csv · SVG
C. Firm stock (NEC 2018 cross-section) — scale & formality

5. Firm-stock scale outcomes — lag profile, by cohort

Eight scale outcomes: log(# firms), log(employment), log(revenue), log(capital), mean employment per firm, industry diversity, and two size-share extremes. Close overlap of the full / post-2001 / post-2011 lines indicates the lag-strengthening profile is intrinsic to the shock-period cohort — not an artifact of pre-shock incumbents clearing out.

Source: output/tab/robustness_nec.csv + robustness_nec_cohort.csv · SVG

6. Firm formality, credit, demographics — lag profile (full 2018 stock)

Eight composition outcomes — tax-registration, account-keeping, registration, year-round operation, borrowing, formal credit usage, female-led share, and the formality index. Tax registration drops consistently (≈ −3 pp); female-led share rises (≈ +1 pp); formal credit usage barely moves. Cohort-restricted versions of these vars are not available without firm-level microdata.

Source: output/tab/robustness_nec.csv · SVG
