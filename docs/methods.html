Method

Construction of the FX-driven shift-share instrument and three reduced-form specifications, following Khanna et al. (2026).

Shock construction

We follow Khanna et al. (2026) for the Philippines, adapted for Nepal where individual migrant income is unobserved. Migrant counts serve as exposure weights. Let $m$ index municipalities, $d$ destination countries, $t$ years.

\begin{equation} \omega_{md,0} = \frac{N_{md,2001}}{\mathrm{Pop}_{m,2001}} \qquad \mathrm{mig\_intensity}_{m,0} = \sum_d \omega_{md,0} \qquad s_{md,0} = \frac{\omega_{md,0}}{\mathrm{mig\_intensity}_{m,0}} \label{eq:weights} \end{equation}

The destination-country shifter is the FX index relative to 2001:

\begin{equation} \mathrm{fxshock}_{dt} = \frac{\mathrm{FX}_{dt}}{\mathrm{FX}_{d,2001}} \label{eq:shifter} \end{equation}

Aggregating to muni-year level gives three regressors:

\begin{align} \mathrm{fxshock}_{mt} &\;=\; \sum_d s_{md,0} \, \mathrm{fxshock}_{dt} \qquad \text{(share-weighted FX shock)}\\ \mathrm{fxshock\_x\_mig\_intensity}_{mt} &\;=\; \sum_d \omega_{md,0} \, \mathrm{fxshock}_{dt} \;=\; \mathrm{mig\_intensity}_{m,0}\,\cdot\,\mathrm{fxshock}_{mt}\\ \mathrm{absexp}_{mt} &\;=\; \sum_d N_{md,2001}\,\mathrm{fxshock}_{dt} \end{align}

The two shocks used in the explorer are fxshock (composition-only, share-weighted) and fxshock_x_mig_intensity (the SSIV — the fxshock scaled by per-capita baseline migration intensity). All treatments are z-scored on the working sample at the muni-year level before estimation, so β is the response per 1 SD of the standardised treatment.

Specification building blocks

The two test pages (Family A and Family B) build their specifications from a common set of components. Each spec is fully described by a treatment term plus an on/off toggle for three control blocks. All specifications include municipality (or HH) and year fixed effects $\alpha_i + \gamma_t$, and standard errors clustered at the municipality.

Treatment options. The reported $\beta$ in every spec is the coefficient on the treatment column listed below.

  • Log interaction: $\mathrm{fx}_z \cdot \log(\mathrm{mig\_int}_z)$ — the heterogeneous-treatment slope on log mig intensity.
  • Linear interaction: $\mathrm{fx}_z \cdot \mathrm{mig\_int}_z$ — same slope idea but linear in mig intensity.
  • Fx alone: $\mathrm{fx}_z$ — average FX-shock effect with no heterogeneity by mig intensity.

Control blocks — fully expanded with year-specific coefficients. Let $\boldsymbol{\tau}_t = (\mathbf{1}\{t=t_1\},\ldots,\mathbf{1}\{t=t_T\})'$ denote the period-FE vector with the reference year ($t_{\text{ref}}=$ 2001 for census, 2016 for HH/plot) omitted. Each block carries one separate coefficient per (variable × non-reference year), matching Khanna et al. (2026) eq. (4) and footnote 12 — i.e. $\mathrm{MigInc}_{o0}\times\mathbf{D}_t$ and $\mathrm{Rshock}_o\times\mathbf{D}_t$ are estimated with year-specific slopes, not collapsed onto a single Post indicator.

\begin{align} C_{\text{mig}} &\;=\; \boldsymbol{\lambda}_1'\bigl(\mathrm{mig\_int}_z\,\boldsymbol{\tau}_t\bigr) \;\equiv\; \sum_{t\neq t_{\text{ref}}} \lambda_{1,t}\,\mathrm{mig\_int}_z\,\mathbf{1}\{t\} \\[4pt] C_{\text{fx}} &\;=\; \boldsymbol{\lambda}_2'\bigl(\mathrm{fx}_z\,\boldsymbol{\tau}_t\bigr) \;\equiv\; \sum_{t\neq t_{\text{ref}}} \lambda_{2,t}\,\mathrm{fx}_z\,\mathbf{1}\{t\} \\[4pt] C_{X} &\;=\; \sum_{k=1}^{K}\boldsymbol{\delta}_k'\bigl(X_{k,m(i),0}\,\boldsymbol{\tau}_t\bigr) \;\equiv\; \sum_{k=1}^{K}\sum_{t\neq t_{\text{ref}}} \delta_{k,t}\,X_{k,m(i),0}\,\mathbf{1}\{t\} \end{align}

$C_{\text{mig}}$ controls for differential trends correlated with baseline migration intensity. $C_{\text{fx}}$ allows year-specific FX shock effects (relaxes constancy across years). $C_{X}$ is Khanna's Block A — the $K$ baseline destination characteristics, namely the six (non-omitted) destination-region migrant shares and the destination-weighted 2001 GDP per capita. For census P3 (2001/11/21, ref=2001) this expands to $2 + 2 + 7\!\times\!2 = 18$ control coefficients in the saturated specification.

Note on Khanna et al. (2026): their eq. (4) uses $\mathbf{X}_{o,0}\times\mathrm{Post}_t$ for the baseline-X term — i.e. one $\delta_k$ per characteristic, not one per (characteristic × period). They have a single pre-shock period (1997 AFC) and multiple post periods, so $\mathrm{Post}_t$ is the natural collapse. For Nepal the FX series moves continuously 2001–2023 with no single-event pre/post boundary, so we keep the more flexible $\mathbf{X}_{o,0}\times\mathbf{D}_t$ form. Setting $\delta_{k,2011}=\delta_{k,2021}$ recovers Khanna's $\times\mathrm{Post}$ as a constrained special case.

All treatment quantities (fx, mig_int, log mig_int) are z-scored on the working sample at the muni-year level before estimation, so $\beta$ is the response per 1 SD of the treatment.

Family A — Cumulative builds (A1 → A4)

Family A is the headline ladder used on the Results page. It holds the treatment fixed at the log-interaction heterogeneous slope ($\mathrm{fx}_z \cdot \log(\mathrm{mig\_int}_z)$) and adds control blocks one at a time. All four columns report the same parameter, so the reader can scan left-to-right and see whether the slope survives as the model saturates.

For the linear-interaction variant ($\mathrm{fx}_z \cdot \mathrm{mig\_int}_z$) and other robustness tweaks (drop one of $C_{\text{mig}}$ / $C_{\text{fx}}$ / $C_X$, or no interaction at all), see Test B (Family B).

\begin{equation} \textbf{A1 — treatment only:}\quad y_{it} = \beta\,\bigl[\mathrm{fx}_z \cdot \log\mathrm{mig\_int}_z\bigr] + \alpha_i + \gamma_t + \varepsilon_{it} \label{eq:a1} \end{equation} \begin{equation} \textbf{A2 — A1 + }C_{\text{mig}}\textbf{:}\quad y_{it} = \beta\,\bigl[\mathrm{fx}_z \cdot \log\mathrm{mig\_int}_z\bigr] + \boldsymbol{\lambda}_1'(\mathrm{mig\_int}_z\,\boldsymbol{\tau}_t) + \alpha_i + \gamma_t + \varepsilon_{it} \label{eq:a2} \end{equation} \begin{equation} \textbf{A3 — A2 + }C_{\text{fx}}\textbf{:}\quad y_{it} = \beta\,\bigl[\mathrm{fx}_z \cdot \log\mathrm{mig\_int}_z\bigr] + \boldsymbol{\lambda}_1'(\mathrm{mig\_int}_z\,\boldsymbol{\tau}_t) + \boldsymbol{\lambda}_2'(\mathrm{fx}_z\,\boldsymbol{\tau}_t) + \alpha_i + \gamma_t + \varepsilon_{it} \label{eq:a3} \end{equation} \begin{equation} \textbf{A4 (saturated) — A3 + }C_{X}\textbf{:}\quad y_{it} = \beta\,\bigl[\mathrm{fx}_z \cdot \log\mathrm{mig\_int}_z\bigr] + \boldsymbol{\lambda}_1'(\mathrm{mig\_int}_z\,\boldsymbol{\tau}_t) + \boldsymbol{\lambda}_2'(\mathrm{fx}_z\,\boldsymbol{\tau}_t) + \boldsymbol{\delta}'(\mathbf{X}_{m(i),0}\,\boldsymbol{\tau}_t) + \alpha_i + \gamma_t + \varepsilon_{it} \label{eq:a4} \end{equation}

What each estimates.

SpecEstimandInterpretation
A1$\beta$ in eq. \eqref{eq:a1}Naïve heterogeneous slope: extra outcome response per 1 SD fx, scaled by 1 SD of log mig intensity, after only entity + year FE.
A2$\beta$ in eq. \eqref{eq:a2}A1 net of differential trends correlated with mig intensity. Removes the "high-mig munis would have moved differently anyway" story.

$\boldsymbol{\tau}_t = (\mathbf{1}\{t=t_1\},\dots,\mathbf{1}\{t=t_T\})'$ is the year-period indicator vector relative to a reference year (2001 for the census, 2016 for the HH and plot panels).

Sample threshold

The threshold dropdown restricts the sample to municipalities with at least k total migrants in 2001 ($k\in\{0,25,50,100\}$). $k=0$ keeps the full panel; raising $k$ trims thin-coverage municipalities and re-z-scores the treatments on the working sample.
